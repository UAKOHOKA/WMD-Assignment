About The Herero People

History and geography

The Herero people are a Bantu ethnic group primarily found in Namibia, Southern Africa with significant populations also found in Botswana and Angola. They are known for their resilience, rich traditions, and unique cultural identity.

The Herero culture is strongly connected to cattle farming, traditional dress, and community values. Their history is an important part of Southern African heritage.

Preserving our heritage

As generations change, preservation is vital to ensure that the Herero language and cultural values are passed down to the future generations, maintaining our identity in a changing world.
